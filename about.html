Project Overview

This smart dashboard examines housing affordability in Seattle through three lenses: rent burden (the share of renter households spending more than 50% of income on housing), renter household income distribution, and the spatial coverage of Mandatory Housing Affordability (MHA) zoning policy. By mapping MHA zones alongside trend data spanning 2006–2022, the dashboard gives residents, advocates, and policymakers a spatial and temporal view of housing inequality in one of the fastest-growing cities in the U.S.

This project was built for GEOG 458: Advanced Digital Geographies at the University of Washington, Winter 2025.

Project Goal

The central question driving this project is: Where has Seattle implemented Mandatory Housing Affordability zoning, and is the citywide rent burden improving or worsening over time — and for whom? Rather than presenting a policy evaluation, this is an exploratory tool — designed to surface spatial and demographic patterns that are hard to see in tables or reports alone.
